## The Ultimate Buying Guide: Choosing Your Perfect Latex Pillow

### 1\. Latex Type: Dunlop vs. Talalay

**Dunlop Latex:** Denser, firmer, more substantial feel. Often considered more durable. Used by Peace Lily and Ecosa.

**Talalay Latex:** Softer, bouncier, more breathable. Creates a consistent, open-cell structure. Used by Dunlopillo and TLC Latex.

### 2\. Firmness and Profile

**Stomach Sleepers:** Need soft, low-profile pillows.

**Back Sleepers:** Need medium-profile, medium-firm pillows.

**Side Sleepers:** Need high-profile, firm pillows to fill the gap between shoulder and head. Body frame also influences profile needs.

### 3\. Breathability and Cooling

**Pincore Holes:** Enhance air circulation (e.g., Dunlopillo, Getha, Ecosa).

**Cooling Infusions/Covers:** Gel infusions (Origin Coolmax) and Tencel covers improve temperature regulation.

### 4\. Health Benefits

**Hypoallergenic & Anti-microbial:** Natural latex resists dust mites, mould, and bacteria, ideal for allergy/asthma sufferers.

**Low-Tox:** Brands like Peace Lily focus on eco-certified latex free from harsh chemicals.

### 5\. Durability and Value for Money

Latex pillows are an investment, typically lasting 10-15 years, making them cost-effective long-term.

### 6\. Trial Periods and Warranties

Some brands offer trial periods (e.g., Peace Lily's 100 nights) and warranties for peace of mind, ensuring your investment is protected.
