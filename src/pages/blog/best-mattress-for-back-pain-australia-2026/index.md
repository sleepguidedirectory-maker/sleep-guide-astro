The Science: What Actually Helps Back Pain  
Research shows medium-firm mattresses work best for back pain. Not soft. Not extra-firm.  
Medium-firm.  
Here's why:

Too Soft = Your spine curves unnaturally When you sink too deep, your hips drop lower  
than your shoulders. Your spine twists. Your muscles strain all night trying to compensate.  
You wake up sore.  
Too Firm = Pressure points scream Rock-hard mattresses don't cushion your shoulders and  
hips. You get pressure buildup. Your lower back arches unnaturally. Your muscles can't  
relax.  
Medium-Firm = The Goldilocks zone Enough cushioning to relieve pressure points.  
Enough support to keep your spine aligned. Your muscles actually rest. You wake up feeling  
better.  
3 in 5 Australians have woken with back pain. If your mattress is over 7-10 years old, it's  
almost certainly making things worse.

Common Questions  
Will a new mattress cure my back pain? If your mattress is old or the wrong firmness,  
yes—upgrading can dramatically reduce pain. But if your pain is from an injury, medical  
condition, or structural issue, a mattress helps but won't cure it.

How long until I notice a difference? Give it 30 days. Your body needs time to adjust.  
Some people feel better immediately. Others need 2-3 weeks.  
What if the mattress makes my pain worse? Return it. Every mattress here has a 100-120  
night trial. If it's not working by day 60, send it back.  
Do I need a mattress topper? No. A good mattress doesn't need a topper. Toppers are band- 
aids for bad mattresses. Save your money and buy the right mattress.  
Should I get memory foam or hybrid? Memory foam: Better pressure relief, more  
contouring, traps more heat Hybrid: Better support, cooler, more responsive  
For back pain, either works. Choose based on temperature preference and whether you like  
"sinking in" or "sleeping on top."  
What about orthopaedic mattresses? "Orthopaedic" is mostly marketing. There's no  
universal agreement on optimal mattress design for back pain. Focus on medium-firm, quality  
materials, and proper trial periods.

The Trial Period: Actually Use It  
Every mattress here has 100-120 nights. That's not a suggestion—it's your safety net.  
Week 1-2: Feels weird. Your body is adjusting. Don't judge yet.  
Week 3-4: Starting to feel normal. Notice if pain is improving or staying the same.  
Week 5-8: This is the truth zone. Is your back better? Same? Worse?  
Week 9+: If you're still in pain, return it. No shame. The mattress isn't working.  
Most people give up after 2 weeks because "it doesn't feel right." That's too soon. Your  
muscles need time to adjust to new support.
